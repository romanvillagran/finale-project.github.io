Pho

Pho is a popular street food that originated in northern Vietnam during the 20th century. The dish consist of meat, rice noodles and herbs, all served in a bone broth flavored with star anise, ginger, black cardamom, coriander, and clove.

While I did say that the dish originated in the 20th century, historians don't really know where Pho came from. Historians believe Pho came from the Nam Ding province in the Red River Delta during the late 19th to early 20th century when the country was still under french control.

Some theorized that Pho is a Vietnamese spin off of the French dish pot-au-feu, which is a one-pot beef vegetable stew or niurou fen, a Chinese beef noodle soup.
